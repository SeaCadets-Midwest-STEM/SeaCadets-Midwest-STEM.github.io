## Overview

The Maker & Invention program puts tools in cadets' hands and challenges them to create something real. Through project-based courses, cadets learn how modern devices are designed and built — from concept through working prototype. These experiences develop practical engineering skills, creative problem-solving, and the confidence to turn ideas into reality.

Courses are hosted at Indiana University in partnership with university faculty and Navy engineers, giving cadets access to university-level facilities and mentorship.

## Courses

### Indiana University Invention Lab

A five-day, build-focused experience where cadets design and construct their own working intelligent device. Using a development platform equipped with sensors, interactive displays, and wireless capability, cadets create a device that senses its surroundings, responds to input, and communicates with other systems.

**What you'll learn:**

- Device design and prototyping
- Sensors and electronics fundamentals
- User interface design
- Basic AI-driven features
- Wireless connectivity and system communication
- Team-based project development

No prior experience is required — curiosity, creativity, and a willingness to learn are essential.
