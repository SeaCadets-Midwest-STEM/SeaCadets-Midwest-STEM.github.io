Indiana University Invention Lab (The 104Pixel Future) is a hands-on, five-day, build-focused STEM experience for cadets who enjoy creating, experimenting, and bringing ideas to life. Training in university facilities with faculty and program leaders, cadets learn how modern intelligent devices are designed and built, then develop their own working prototype.

Throughout the week, cadets use a versatile development platform equipped with sensors, interactive displays, and wireless capability. They design a device that senses its surroundings, responds to input, and communicates with other systems. Projects incorporate user interface design, basic AI-driven features, and wireless connectivity in a structured, team-based setting.

No prior experience is required. Curiosity, creativity, and a willingness to learn are essential.

The Indiana University + Sea Cadet program is the only one of its kind, designed specifically for Sea Cadets by a major research university and Navy engineers. Cadets have a unique opportunity to experience university-level STEM training tied directly to real-world Navy missions.

## Requirements

- Must be an active Sea Cadet
- Minimum age is 14 due to university requirements
- Open to all experience levels
