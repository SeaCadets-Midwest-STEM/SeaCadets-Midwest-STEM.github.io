Indiana University Basic CyberImmersion & Electronic Warfare is a hands-on experience hosted by IU's Hamilton Lugar School of Global and International Studies and Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering.

Training alongside university faculty and engineers from Naval Surface Warfare Center (NSWC) Crane, cadets explore how digital systems, devices, and signals are protected in real operational environments. Through team-based, practical challenges, they examine how signals are generated, detected, and defended in the electromagnetic spectrum.

Topics include ethical hacking, Internet of Things (IoT) security, data breaches and cloud data protection, cybersecurity policy and national security, protecting critical infrastructure, signal exploration and spectrum fundamentals, and AI/LLM applications. Cadets apply these concepts in mission-based cybersecurity and electronic warfare scenarios.

No prior cyber experience is required. Curiosity, motivation, and teamwork are essential.

The Indiana University + Sea Cadet program is the only one of its kind, designed specifically for Sea Cadets by a major research university and Navy engineers. Cadets have a unique opportunity to experience university-level STEM training tied directly to real-world Navy missions.

- Minimum age is 14 due to university requirements
