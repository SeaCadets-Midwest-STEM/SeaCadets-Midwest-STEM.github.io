Indiana University Advanced CyberImmersion & Electronic Warfare is a next-level experience for cadets ready to build on prior knowledge. It is hosted by IU's Hamilton Lugar School of Global and International Studies and Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering.

Training with university faculty and Naval Surface Warfare Center (NSWC) Crane engineering/cyber professionals, cadets deepen their understanding of how cyber operations and electronic warfare function in operational environments. Advanced cadets take ownership of mission execution—designing defensive strategies, analyzing real-time data, and adapting to evolving threat conditions.

Topics include network defense and active monitoring, adversary simulation and red/blue team exercises, secure systems architecture, signals analysis, spectrum management, cyber mission planning, vulnerability assessment, and applied electronic warfare problem sets. Cadets will work through complex, multi-day mission scenarios that require technical execution, strategic thinking, and disciplined teamwork under time pressure. The experience emphasizes leadership in technical teams and decision-making in ambiguous situations.

This course is designed for cadets with prior experience in cybersecurity, coding, robotics, or other STEM activities.

The Indiana University + Sea Cadet program is the only one of its kind, designed specifically for Sea Cadets by a major research university and Navy engineers. Cadets have a unique opportunity to experience university-level STEM training tied directly to real-world Navy missions.

## Requirements

- Must be an active Sea Cadet
- Minimum age is 14 due to university requirements
- Prior STEM experience such as the Basic Cybersecurity course or CyberPatriot is necessary to benefit from this training
